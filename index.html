Munnar

Munnar, a picturesque hill station in Kerala, is renowned for its endless tea plantations, mist-covered hills, and serene atmosphere. Visitors can explore the Eravikulam National Park, trek through lush landscapes, and experience the charm of colonial-era bungalows. Munnar is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Alleppey

Alleppey, often called the "Venice of the East," is famous for its scenic backwaters, tranquil houseboat cruises, and picturesque lagoons. Tourists can enjoy a unique experience drifting along serene waters, witnessing lush greenery, and experiencing Kerala’s rich culture and local cuisine. It's the perfect escape for a peaceful retreat.

Wayanad

Wayanad, a pristine region in the Western Ghats, boasts breathtaking waterfalls, mysterious caves, and vast spice plantations. The district is home to exotic wildlife, historical ruins, and adventurous trekking trails. With its untouched natural beauty and refreshing climate, Wayanad is a must-visit destination for eco-tourism enthusiasts.

Kovalam

Kovalam, Kerala’s most famous beach destination, is known for its crescent-shaped coastline, golden sands, and vibrant nightlife. The iconic lighthouse, Ayurvedic wellness centers, and thrilling water sports attract visitors from all over the world. Kovalam provides a perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and cultural experiences by the sea.

Thekkady

Thekkady, home to the renowned Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, offers a thrilling jungle experience with its diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy boat safaris on Periyar Lake, spot elephants in their natural habitat, and explore spice plantations. Thekkady is a perfect getaway for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts.
